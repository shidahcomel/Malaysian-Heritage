Malay Traditional Attires



Baju Melayu:
A Baju Melayu is a traditional Malay outfit worn by Malay men, particularly in Malaysia and Brunei, often for formal events, religious occasions, or cultural celebrations. The ensemble consists of a long-sleeved tunic, which is worn with a matching pair of trousers. The tunic features a high collar and is often paired with a "songkok" (a traditional black cap) and a "samping," a decorative cloth wrapped around the waist. The Baju Melayu is known for its simple yet elegant design, and its subtle colors and patterns vary depending on the occasion, with brighter shades reserved for celebrations like Eid. It is a symbol of Malay heritage and is widely recognized as a national attire in Malaysia.


Baju Kurung:
A Baju Kurung is a traditional Malaysian outfit worn by women, particularly among the Malay community. It consists of a loose-fitting, long-sleeved blouse and a matching knee-length or floor-length skirt. The ensemble is often made from luxurious fabrics like silk, cotton, or brocade and is known for its modest yet elegant design. The Baju Kurung is typically worn during formal occasions such as weddings, religious festivals, and cultural events, and it symbolizes traditional Malay femininity. The outfit is highly versatile and can be styled with accessories like a "selendang" (shawl) or a headscarf, depending on personal preference or occasion. The Baju Kurung has remained an iconic piece of Malaysian national dress, reflecting the country's cultural heritage and modest fashion.

Saree:
A Malaysian Saree are often referred to as a "sari" by locals. This outfit is originated from India, this clothing blends traditional Indian saree aesthetics with Malaysian cultural influences. It typically features vibrant colors, intricate patterns, and luxurious fabrics like silk or brocade. The Malaysian saree incorporates elements from both Malay and Indian cultures, showcasing a fusion of styles that cater to the diverse Malaysian population and not too revealing fashion. This garment is worn on special occasions or daily, symbolizing elegance, tradition, and the rich cultural heritage of both India and Malaysia.

Chinese Traditional Attires



Samfoo:
Samfoo is a traditional Chinese outfit, commonly worn by men, consisting of a loose-fitting, short-sleeved tunic and matching trousers. The tunic is typically buttoned up with a mandarin collar and made from fabrics such as silk, cotton, or brocade, often adorned with subtle Chinese motifs or embroidery. The samfoo, though less formal than the cheongsam, still reflects Chinese heritage and has been adapted with Southeast Asian influences. Popular in Malaysia among the Chinese community, the male samfoo is worn during casual events, cultural celebrations, or festivals like Chinese New Year. Its relaxed, comfortable design made it a popular choice for everyday wear, though it is also worn for traditional and family gatherings, representing both cultural pride and practical style.

Cheongsam:
Cheongsam, also known as the Qipao, is a traditional Chinese dress worn by women, often during cultural celebrations, weddings, and festive occasions such as Chinese New Year. The Cheongsam is characterized by its close-fitting silhouette, high collar, and side slits, offering an elegant yet flattering fit. In Malaysia, the dress has been adapted with local influences, often featuring vibrant colors, intricate embroidery, or modern fabrics like silk and satin. The Cheongsam is typically worn by the Malaysian Chinese community, symbolizing cultural pride and sophistication.

Iban Traditional Attires

Kain Sirat & Dangdong:
Iban men's traditional clothing is divided into two, Kain Sirat and Dandong. Sirat cloth, also known as loin cloth, is the basic clothing of the Iban people. Sirat is worn by wrapping it around the waist and crotch. The ends of this cloth hang from the waist to the front and back of the body, showing off the decorated part. Dandong is a scarf fabric worn by Iban men. Dandong is wider than swaddling. The length of the dandong is not fixed because this cloth is used as a wrapper for offerings in certain ceremonies.
